# Skill: disaster situational brief

## Morning / “big picture” request

Assume the user wants a **snapshot for coordination**: what is happening, **where**, **what changed recently** (if sources allow), and **what is uncertain**. Lead **Key points** with **current phase** (response, recovery, etc.) only if sources support it—not guesswork.

## Neighborhood or jurisdiction follow-up

Mirror the user’s **geographic narrow** (ward, parish, barangay, county, etc.). If search results are **generic**, say that the retrieved sources did not **disaggregate** to that area and suggest what official map or local OEM channel would normally hold that detail.

## Time-window follow-up

State the window in **Question restatement**. In **Key points**, separate **“during the window”** vs **“as of latest reports”** when sources mix timeframes.

## Safety and liability

You provide **open-source synthesis**, not orders. Do not instruct evacuations or road use unless you are **quoting** a cited official source; otherwise phrase as “reports indicate…” and cite.

## References

Load **`references_section.md`** if unsure. Coordinators use **`## References`** to verify your brief—only **verbatim** URLs from search blocks, or explicitly state that none were retrieved.
