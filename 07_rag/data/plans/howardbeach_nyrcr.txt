The NY Rising Community Reconstruction Program is helping
communities impacted by Hurricane Irene, Tropical Storm Lee, and
Superstorm Sandy to rebuild and become more resilient through
community-driven plans that consider current damage, future threats
to community assets, and the community’s economic future. Residents
are being asked to participate in public forums to learn more about
the program and share their input on assets, needs, opportunities,
and community vision to help shape the planning process.

NY Rising Community Reconstruction Program

While Superstorm Sandy was an epic storm that carried a significant flooding impact,
many low-lying sections of Howard Beach flood routinely during equinox tides.

The Howard Beach community consists of multiple residential neighborhoods that are
separated by large inlets and basins.

Much of the waterfront edge of the Howard Beach community is comprised of park
space, much of it is natural and not accessible to the community. When developing
resiliency strategies, consider opportunities to increase access to active and passive
recreation.
